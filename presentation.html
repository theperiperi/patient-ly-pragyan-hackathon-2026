Problem Statement

Emergency Triage is Broken

Triage nurses spend 10–15 minutes manually assembling patient context from 6+ disconnected systems — ambulance reports on paper, EHRs, wearables, labs, handwritten referrals. The problem isn't a lack of data. It's a lack of interoperability.

The Interoperability Gap
A heart rate from a Fitbit is structurally incompatible with a pulse reading in an EMR. Ambulance NEMSIS data can't merge with hospital HL7 records. Handwritten notes sit in paper charts. Without a unified semantic layer, clinicians make life-or-death decisions with fragmented information.
Why Existing Solutions Fail
• EMR integrations only connect within one hospital's ecosystem
• HIE networks require months of setup and don't handle real-time data
• No solution ingests wearables, ambulance telemetry, and scanned notes together
• No AI triage system works across all 6 source types simultaneously

Proposed Solution

A Cognitive Triage Ecosystem

Patient.ly replaces manual data-gathering with an end-to-end intelligent pipeline — ingesting, standardizing, and reasoning over patient data to deliver triage recommendations in under 90 seconds.

AI Ingestion

Voice Agents & Vision AI

Voice-Based Input Multilingual Support

Paramedics need hands-free data entry. Doctors' handwriting defeats traditional OCR. Patient.ly solves both with AI-native pipelines that output structured FHIR resources.

Voice Agent Pipeline

Nurses and paramedics dictate observations hands-free. The system transcribes, extracts structured data, and generates FHIR resources automatically.

Vision Language Models

Beyond OCR — VLMs read handwritten prescriptions with contextual understanding, inferring medications, dosages, and clinical intent.

Voice Example
“Patient is a 57-year-old male, chest pain radiating to left arm, onset two hours ago, HR 108, SpO2 94...”
→ Auto-generates: Encounter, 4 Observations (HR, SpO2, BP, RR), Condition (chest pain SNOMED code), all linked to patient MRN
Vision Example
Scanned referral letter with handwritten vitals, medication list, and diagnosis
→ Gemini Vision extracts: 3 Conditions, 5 Medications with dosages, DocumentReference with original scan attached as base64
Why This Beats Traditional OCR
• Understands medical context, not just characters
• Infers implicit information (drug classes, urgency)
• Outputs structured FHIR, not raw text
• Multi-language support (Hindi, Tamil, English)

Intelligence

Hybrid Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning

Bias & Fairness

No black boxes. Four intelligence layers where deterministic safety rules form the base and probabilistic AI adds insight. Each layer can escalate priority but never downgrade it.

4
Agentic Reasoning — LLM Agents
Gemini / GPT-4o synthesize unstructured data, flag subtle findings for specialist protocols.
3
ML Prediction — XGBoost
23 triage-time features, 87% F1 accuracy. Top features: encounter recency, heart rate, temperature.
2
Clinical Scoring — NEWS2
6-parameter physiological scoring. Score ≥7 auto-triggers high-priority alert.
1
Safety Protocols — ESI Rules
Deterministic life-threat detection. SpO2 < 90% or GCS < 8 forces ESI-1 regardless of AI layers.
SHAP Explainability: Every classification includes feature-level explanations. Clinicians can validate and override any recommendation.
Bias & Fairness: Model audited across age, gender, and chief complaint subgroups. Weighted sampling corrects for ESI class imbalance. No demographic feature used in prediction.

Pre-Hospital Integration

The Smart Ambulance Bridge

Real-Time Simulation

Triage starts before arrival. Patient.ly streams real-time ambulance ePCR data via NEMSIS-to-FHIR so the AI begins assessment during transport.

Emergency Dispatch
108 receives call, ambulance dispatched with GPS tracking activated
ePCR Data Capture
Paramedic records vitals, chief complaint, GCS, and interventions on tablet
Real-Time Streaming
Vitals + location transmitted via WebSocket to hospital NEMSIS → FHIR pipeline
Pre-Arrival AI Assessment
Hospital AI runs initial triage, alerts specialist teams, reserves resources (CT, OR)
Seamless Handoff
Patient record fully pre-populated in the clinical dashboard on arrival — zero re-entry

Why This Matters

In a suspected stroke case, pre-arrival data allows the system to alert the neurology team and reserve the CT scanner before the ambulance arrives. This can reduce door-to-needle time by critical minutes, directly impacting patient survival and recovery outcomes.

NEMSIS v3.5 • FHIR R4 • WebSocket • GPS Telemetry
Throughput: ~200 concurrent ambulance streams per node

23 Triage-Time Features
Demographics, initial vitals (HR, SpO2, BP, temp, RR), chief complaint SNOMED codes, patient encounter history, temporal context. Only features available at patient arrival.
Zero Temporal Leakage
Uses FIRST vital measurement only (not post-treatment). Current encounter excluded from patient history. No outcome features in training data.
SNOMED-Based Labeling
26 clinically mapped SNOMED codes (e.g. MI → ESI-1, Seizure → ESI-2), refined by vital sign thresholds. Not synthetic formulas.
Class-Balanced Training
Weighted sampling across imbalanced ESI distribution (111 patients, 60/20/20 split). XGBoost with early stopping at 200 estimators, depth 6.
